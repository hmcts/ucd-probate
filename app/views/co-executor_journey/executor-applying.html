Executors applying for probate

Up to 4 executors, including yourself, can be named on the application and the grant of probate.

All executors will be given the legal right to deal with Jane Elizabeth Doe’s estate. This includes:

  • collecting the estate’s assets (property, money and possessions)
  • paying any taxes and debts
  • distributing what is left to the people entitled to it (the ‘beneficiaries’)

We will contact the other executors and ask them to check the information you have provided in this application and confirm that it is correct.

If an executor does not want to apply

If any executor does not want to deal with the estate, they will have to either:

  • reserve the right to apply later – this is known as holding ‘power reserved’ and they will have to inform you of this in writing
  • permanently give up their right to apply – this is known as ‘renunciation’ and they will have to fill in a form to do this
